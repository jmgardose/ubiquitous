About

History

It was founded in Yokohama, Japan

Ubiquitous Technologies is an IT company based in Yokohama, Japan founded by Toshiharu Kai together with Keiichi Tanaka in 2005.

Established with a paid-in capital of 10,000,000 yen in the business of software development for electronic components and sales of such product. The capital doubled in amount after the year ended and continued to gradually increase in the succeeding years.

Build up in Makati, Philippines

Toshiharu Kai desired more for the company and did not stop there.
With a notable background of working in the Philippines, Toshiharu Kai decided to expand the business and opened an office in Makati City, Philippines in 2006 for the main purpose of establishing an "engineering and research center for software development".

Ubiquitous Technologies Philippines, Inc. was established with a paid-in capital of 14,000,000 yen. The capital continued to gradually increase in the succeeding years.

Further expansion in Kyushu, Japan

Toshiharu Kai met with Takeshi Yamamoto and decided to open Ubiquitous Kyushu Development Center in 2014, to focus on Factory Automation business expansion.

All hardware, mechanical and electric components are being built in Kyushu. Takeshi Yamamoto became the Factory Automation division operation officer.

Up to this moment, Ubiquitous Technologies continues to develop and grow to conquer challenges that comes in its way. Ubiquitous Technologies aims to become the driving force of Japan and contribute to society globally in the future with various technologies to support the software and hardware manufacturing industry.

Founders

Toshiharu Kai

CEO & Founder

He had a long-year experience in software development of mobile communication terminals.

Since 1998, he had an extensive technical experience on project management, process improvement and originative development of various software development support tools as a project manager on software development for i-Mode cellular phones.

In year 2004, as the main in-charge to i-Mode cellular phone development group, he had headed the group to achieve CMM Level 4 on institutionalising the CMM practices into the software development process in which the certificate is awarded by CMU/SEI accredited lead assessors.

He is the editorial supervisor of the book "図解 よくわかるソフトウェア・ジャストインタイム"
