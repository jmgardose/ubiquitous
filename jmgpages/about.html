History

It was all founded in Yokohama Japan

Ubiquitous Technologies is an IT company based in Yokohama Japan, founded by Toshiharu Kai together with Keiichiâ€‚Tanaka in 2005.

Established with paid-in capital of 10,000,000 yen and with main business of software development for electronics component and sales of such product.

Build up in Makati Philippines

Toshiharu Kai desired more for the company and did not stop there.
With a notable background of working in the Philippines, Toshiharu Kai decided to expand the business and opened an office in Makati City, Philippines in 2006 for the main purpose of establishing an "Engineering and Research" center for software development.

Ubiquitous Technologies Philippines, Inc. was established with paid-in capital of 4,000,000 yen. The capital fund increased to five (5) folds before the year ended and continued to gradually increase in the succeeding years.

Further expansion in Kyushu Japan

Toshiharu Kai met with Takeshi Yamamoto and decided to opened Ubiquitous Kyushu Development Center in 2014, to focus on Factory Automation business expansion. All hardware, mechanical and electric components are being built in Kyushu. Takeshi Yamamoto became the Factory Automation division operation officer.

Up to this moment, Ubiquitous Technologies continues to develop and conquer challenges that comes in its way. Ubiquitous Technologies will make things in order to become the driving force of Japan and will contribute to society in the future with various technologies to support the manufacturing industry.
